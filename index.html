About Me
Agricultural Engineer
meets AI Researcher

I graduated with a Bachelor's in Agricultural Engineering from Tribhuvan University, Institute of Engineering (First Division — 3.56 GPA international equivalent) and have been working at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, and climate-smart farming ever since.

My work focuses on building AI systems that address real agricultural challenges in Nepal — where data is scarce, climate is variable, and the stakes for farmers are high. I believe technology should be grounded in field reality, not just benchmarks.

My research on meteorology-driven crop yield prediction was presented at ICRTAI 2025. I also completed a research internship at CREEW working on climate impacts on crop phenology and spatial data analysis using QGIS.

Currently seeking MS/PhD opportunities in AI for Agriculture, Precision Agriculture, and Remote Sensing.

Full ML pipeline predicting district-level crop yields across Nepal using NASA POWER meteorological data and MoALD agricultural statistics. XGBoost outperformed all other models. Deployed as a live Streamlit web app.
